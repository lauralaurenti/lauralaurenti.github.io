About / Bio

I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at ETH Zürich. My work focuses on the intersection of deep learning and geophysical signals. I am particularly interested in how self-supervised learning can reveal hidden patterns in seismic data.

Previously, I completed my PhD where I developed neural architectures for real-time earthquake early warning systems. I focus on merging physical constraints with data-driven models to improve predictive accuracy.

Research Interests

My current research agenda involves Neural Seismology, where we leverage Large Language Models (LLMs) and Transformer architectures to classify seismic phases. I am also exploring Physics-Informed Neural Networks to ensure that AI-generated predictions adhere to fundamental physical laws.
