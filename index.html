Merging Deep Learning with Geophysical Data to predict the heartbeat of the Earth.

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at ETH Zürich within the Department of Earth Sciences. My research lies at the intersection of seismology and machine learning, focusing on the development of neural architectures capable of processing massive seismic catalogs.

I am currently developing self-supervised learning models to detect low-magnitude micro-seismic events that traditional algorithms often miss. My goal is to enhance early-warning systems and improve our understanding of fault mechanics using AI.

Selected Research

Neural Waveform Analysis

Utilizing Transformers to identify phase arrivals in high-noise environments, outperforming classical STA/LTA methods by 40%.

Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs)

Integrating elastodynamic equations into the loss functions of CNNs to ensure geophysically consistent predictions.
